Section 2.3: Models and Applications

Mixture Problems

Example 1:

A coffee distributor needs to mix a(n) Costa Rican coffee bland than normally sells for $ 11.60 per pound with a Terraza coffee blend that normally sells for $14.30 per pound to create 70 pounds of a coffee that can sell for $13.18 per pound. How many pounds of each kind of coffee should the mix?




Example 2:

A candy distributor needs to mix a 20% fat-content chocolate with a 30% fat-content chocolate to create 150 kilograms of 23.3% fat-content chocolate. How many kilograms of each kind of chocolate must they use?




Example 3:

A combined total of $50,000 is invested in two bonds that pay 4% and 9% simple interest. The annual interest is $3,700.00. How much is invested in each bond?




Job Problems

Example 4:

One pump can empty a pool in 5 days, whereas a second pump can empty the same pool in 13 days. How long will it take the two pumps, working together, to empty the pool?




Example 5:

It takes an older employee 11 hours to shovel the snow from a sidewalk, but a younger employee can shovel the same sidewalk in 10 hours. How long will it take them to clear the sidewalk if they work together?




Example 6:

Two welders who work at the same rate are welding the girders of a building. After they work together for 13 hours, one of the welders quites. The second welder requires 24 more hours to complete the welds. Find the time it would have taken one of the welders, working alone, to complete the welds.
