Lesson 7: Sections 2.7: Inequalities

Inequalities

  • \(<\) less than, use open points for graphs and parenthesis for intervals(and sometimes graphs).

  • \(>\) greater than, use open points for graphs and parenthesis for intervals(and sometimes graphs).

  • \(\leq\) less than or equal to, use closed points for graphs and brackets for intervals(and sometimes graphs).

  • \(\geq\) greater than or equal to, use closed points for graphs and brackets for intervals
    (and sometimes graphs).

Note: Infinities (\(\pm \infty\)) never get brackets, only parenthesis.


For examples 1-3, write each inequality in the equivalent interval and graph forms.

Solving Linear Inequalities

  1. Isolate the variable.

  2. If the inequality(ies) was(ere) multiplied or divided by a negative number while isolating the variable, the inequality(ies) must be reversed(flipped).

  3. Write the solution in interval notation. It may be helpful to also sketch the solution graphically.

Absolute Value Inequalities

The two forms involving absolute value inequalities. For \(b>0\).

  1. \(|x|<b\) is equivalent to \(-b<x<b\).

  2. \(|x|>b\) is equivalent to \(x<-b\) or \(x>b\).

Example 8:
