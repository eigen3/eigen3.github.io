The Method of Completing the Square.

To solve \(ax^2+bx+c\) by the method of completing the square:

  1. Identify \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).

  2. Move \(c\) to the other side of the equation.

  3. If \(a \ne 1\), dive the equation by \(a\).

  4. Add \(\left(\dfrac{b}{2a}\right)^2\) to both sides of the equation.

  5. Factor and simplify.

  6. Solve using the square root property(method).

The Quadratic Formula:

If \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), then \(x=\dfrac{-b\pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\).


The Discriminate \(b^2-4ac\) indicates the number and type of zeros(roots) of a quadratic equation.

  • If \(b^2-4ac>0\) and a perfect square, then there are two different rational zeros.

  • If \(b^2-4ac>0\) and not a perfect square, then there are two different irrational zeros.

  • If \(b^2-4ac=0\), then there is one repeated rational zero. Also called a zero of multiplicity two or a double root.

  • If \(b^2-4ac<0\), then there are two complex conjugate zeros.
