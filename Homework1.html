Sections 1.1: Real Numbers: Algebra Essentials

Types of numbers:

  • \(\mathbb{N}\) the natural numbers: \(\mathbb{N}=\{1,2,3,...\}\).
  • \(\mathbb{W}\) the whole numbers: \(\mathbb{W}=\{0,1,2,3,...\}\).
  • \(\mathbb{Z}\) the integers: \(\mathbb{Z}=\{0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \pm 3,...\}\).
  • \(\mathbb{Q}\) the rational numbers: \(\mathbb{Q}=\{\frac{a}{b}: \text{ where } a,b \text{ are integers and } b \ne 0\}\).
  • \(\mathbb{P}\) it irrational numbers cannot be represented as a ratio of two integers.
  • \(\mathbb{R}\) is the set of all rational and irrational numbers: \(\mathbb{R}=(\infty,\infty)\).
  • \(\mathbb{C}\) is the set of complex numbers: \(\mathbb{C}=\{a+bi : \text{ where } a,b \text{ are real numbers} \}\)

Order of Operations:

  1. Parenthesis
  2. Exponents
  3. Multiplication and Division in order from left to right.
  4. Addition and Subtraction in order from left to right.

  1. \(\dfrac{-1+3^2-(-6)}{-4+5-6}\)



  1. \(\left(\dfrac{1}{3}+\dfrac{5}{6}\right) \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}\)



  1. \((4+2)^2-4^2+2^2\)




Section 1.2: Exponents and Scientific Notation

Exponent Rules

Given \(a, b \ne 0\) and \(m, n \in \mathbb{R}\).

  • Product Rule: \(b^m \cdot b^n = b^{m+n}\)

  • Quotient Rule: \(\dfrac{b^m}{b^n}=b^{m-n}\)

  • Power Rule: \((b^m)^n=b^{m \cdot n}\)

  • Zero: \(b^0=1\)

  • Negative Exponent: \(b^{-n}=\dfrac{1}{b^n}\) and \(\dfrac{1}{b^{-n}}=b^n\)

  • Power of a Product: \((ab)^n=a^n b^n\)

  • Power of a Quotient: \(\left(\dfrac{a}{b}\right)^n=\dfrac{a^n}{b^n}\)

  • Fraction to a negative exponent: \(\left(\dfrac{a}{b}\right)^{-n}=\left(\dfrac{b}{a}\right)^n\)

  • Radical to rational exponent: \((\sqrt[n]{b})^m=\sqrt[n]{b^m}=b^\frac{m}{n}\)
